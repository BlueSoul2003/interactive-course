Primary 6 English | Unit 9

The Debate Club

Separate fact from opinion, report what others said, and build balanced arguments for and against.

01Fact or opinion?EVIDENCE

Water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius.

这句话是 fact 还是 opinion？

Can this statement be measured and proven?

Scientific measurements can verify it.

02Name the language functionTONE

"I see your point, but I have a different view."

这句话的沟通功能是什么？

The speaker respects the first idea, then differs.

Look at but I have a different view.

03Choose supporting evidenceDETAIL

Claim: Online practice can support learning.

哪一个细节最能支持这个观点？

The detail must explain a learning benefit.

Look for a sentence about how students learn.

04Change to reported speechREPORT

Maya said, "I enjoy debates."

把直接引语改为 reported speech。

Change I to she and enjoy to enjoyed.

Use said that.

05Trace the pronounREFERENCE

The students presented their ideas. They spoke clearly.

They 指的是谁？

Who can speak?

They refers to a plural group of people.

06Build a debate nounSUFFIX

Add a suffix to agree.

给 agree 加上正确的 suffix，组成名词。

agree-ment-ful-ly-er

The noun suffix is -ment.

Drop one e when joining.

07Read the argumentCLAIM

What is the writer's main argument?

作者的主要观点是什么？

School uniforms can reduce pressure to buy fashionable clothes. They also make it easier to identify pupils during school trips. For these reasons, uniforms can support a more focused school environment.

Which option is supported by the first sentence?

Look for reduce pressure.

08Build both sidesFOR / AGAINST

Should homework be limited?

针对这个辩题，各写一个支持和反对的理由。

FOR

Pupils need time to rest.

AGAINST

Practice helps pupils remember.

Write one reason for.Write one reason against.Explain each reason clearly.

Balanced debate considers both sides.

Use because to explain each reason.

09Email the school councilFINAL SPEECH

Write a persuasive email

给校务委员会写一封有理有据的 email。

Motion: The school should create a weekly reading hour.

State your opinion clearly.Give two supporting reasons.Use a linking phrase such as furthermore.End politely.

Tell the council how reading helps pupils.
