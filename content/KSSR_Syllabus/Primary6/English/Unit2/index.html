🧠 First Principles: "-er" vs "-or"

Rule: Most English action verbs take "-er" to become a person who does that action (teach → teacher).
However, words ending in "-ct", "-ate", or "-it" usually take "-or" (act → actor).

底层逻辑规则： 大多数英语动词加 "-er" 变成做该动作的人（teach → teacher）。但是，以 "-ct", "-ate", "-it" 结尾的词（通常来源于拉丁语）一般加 "-or"（act → actor）。

Past Habits

Learn how to build sentences using "used to" and "didn't use to".

学习如何使用“过去常常”和“过去不常”来造句。

🧱 First Principles: Sentence Formula

To build these sentences, follow a simple formula. Think of it like Lego blocks!

✅ Positive (肯定的): Subject (主语) + used to + Base Verb (动词原形) + Detail (细节)
❌ Negative (否定的): Subject (主语) + didn't use to + Base Verb (动词原形) + Detail (细节)
⚠️ Grammar Secret: Why is there no "d" in "didn't use to"? Because "didn't" already stole the past tense! 语法秘密：为什么“use”没有“d”？因为“didn't”已经把过去式“偷”走了！

Then & Now

Build long compound sentences using "but" to show how habits have changed.

🔄 First Principles: The "But Now" Formula

When someone changes their habit, we join two sentences with ", but now". The first part is about the past, and the second part is about the present!

[Past Habit] + , but now + [Present Habit]
Example: Raju used to live in Kampar, but now he lives in Kuala Lumpur.
底层逻辑规则： 当习惯改变时，我们用 ", but now" (但是现在) 连接两个句子。逗号 "," 很重要，它让句子有一个停顿。第一部分用过去式 (used to)，第二部分用现在式 (lives, does 等)！

Everyday English

Learn how to match formal phrases with casual talk, and master emotional sound words!

👔 First Principles: Formal vs. Informal

Formal language is like wearing a suit. We use it when there is "distance" between us and the listener (e.g., a boss, a principal, or an official letter). It uses longer words.

Informal language is like wearing a t-shirt. We use it when we are "close" to the listener (e.g., friends, family). It uses shorter words and idioms!

底层逻辑规则：
正式用语 (Formal) 就像穿西装，当我们和听众有“距离感”时使用（如老板、校长或公函），通常单词较长。
非正式用语 (Informal) 就像穿T恤，当听众是“亲密”的人时使用（如朋友、家人），通常用简短的词和习语！

💥 First Principles: Interjections (Tone Words)

Interjections are the "sound effects" of human emotion! They don't follow strict grammar rules. They just burst out to show exactly how you feel in one quick sound.
