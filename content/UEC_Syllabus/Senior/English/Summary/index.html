📝 Mission: Procrastination (拖延症)

Your task: Write a summary of not more than 150 words based on the 2017 Past Year Paper.

你的任务：阅读原文，基于2017年历届考题，写一篇不超过150字的摘要。
  • 1. Reasons why people procrastinate (原因)
  • 2. Its ill effects (坏处)
  • 3. How to overcome procrastination (克服方法)
🕹️ Stage 1: Treasure Hunting (实战寻宝)

Mission 1: Find the REASONS (找出原因)

"There are many reasons for procrastinating. The first is that we may dread taking on a difficult or boring job. When a task is perceived as long or difficult, many will procrastinate simply because there is no immediate gratification. For example, it will take months to lose those ten pounds or it will take tons of energy to clear the room. Putting off a diet programme one more day will not make much difference. Sleeping in a messy room for another day won't make a difference, and the list goes on. Besides that, fear of failure is also another reason why we procrastinate. This fear often comes into play at school where performance is evaluated and can affect our future success. A student may put off studying for a test until the last minute. Lack of time spent for studying is a more desirable explanation for failure than lacking the intelligence to learn the material."

List 3 Reasons (找出3个原因)

Mission 2: Find the EFFECTS (找出坏处)

"Procrastination causes stress, anxiety and a feeling of failure. Students who fear failure ultimately become anxious, and stressful, and tend to fail when they do not prepare for tests or start projects on time. Employees who begin tasks at the last minute rarely perform as well as their non-procrastinating colleagues. Putting off a diet programme ultimately adds to the number of pounds you need to lose to be healthy, thus, making the job that much harder to accomplish."

Mission 3: WAYS TO OVERCOME (解决方法)

"To overcome procrastination, first, figure out why you are procrastinating. Is it fear of failure or lack of confidence? Write down all the reasons why putting off your project will bring you one step closer to failure. Does it make sense to put it off any longer? NO. Next, break down the task into smaller jobs. If it is a thirty-page paper due in two weeks, write down a goal of finishing three pages a night. After you have finished your nightly quota, give yourself a reward of some sort. Watch your favourite television programme or have a hearty meal. Those of us who procrastinate on a day-to-day basis need a realistic 'to-do' list. Keep the list with you and stick to the order. Cross things off as they are accomplished. Tell your friends and family about your goals. If you have someone asking you how your diet is coming along, it is more difficult to cheat. If you have a major project to do, ask a friend to help keep you on the task by monitoring you frequently."

Model Answer

🏆 Final Summary (approx. 85 words)

Firstly, people procrastinate because they dread difficult or boring jobs and lack immediate gratification. Moreover, fear of failure is another cause.

Consequently, it leads to stress, anxiety, and a feeling of failure. It also results in poor performance and makes tasks harder to accomplish.

To overcome this, we should figure out the reasons and break tasks into smaller jobs. We can also create a realistic to-do list and give ourselves rewards. Finally, we should ask friends to monitor our progress.

首先，人们拖延是因为害怕困难或无聊的工作，且缺乏即时满足感。此外，害怕失败也是原因之一。因此，这导致压力、焦虑和失败感，还会导致表现不佳，通过使任务更难完成。为了克服这一点，我们应该找出原因并将任务分解为小工作。我们还可以制定现实的待办清单并给自己奖励。最后，我们应该请朋友监督我们的进度。

💡 Teacher's Tips (老师的叮嘱)

  • 🚫 Strictly No Examples: Do not include specific examples like "diet" or "30-page paper". （绝对不要写例子！）
  • ✍️ Use Own Words (Optional): If you can paraphrase "monitor" to "check", great. If not, copying keywords is safer. （能改写最好，不能改写就抄关键词，比较安全。）
  • 🔗 Connectors are Key: Words like Firstly, Moreover, Consequently act as glue. （连接词是关键！）
